All About Ayodhya

Who made Ayodhya
ANS: According to the Ramayana, Ayodhya was founded by Manu, the progenitor of mankind, and measured 12✕3 yojanas in area․ Both Ramayana and Mahabharata describe Ayodhya as capital of Ikshvaku dynasty of Kosala, including Rama and Dasharatha․
Ayodhya is birthplace of shree Rama and setting of the great epic Ramayana․ Ayodhya, also know as Saketa, is an ancient city of India, Ayodhya used to be the capital of the ancient Kosala Kingdom, Ayodhya(Awadhpuri) has been regarded as first one of seven most important pilgrimage sites(Mokshdayini SaptPuri) for Hindus․

Who is the first king of Ayodhya
ANS: Vaivasvata Manu According to Ramayana, Ayodhya was founded by Vaivasvata Manu(son of Lord Surya and descendant of lord Brahma) and first ruler of Ayodhya was Ikshvaku(Son of Vaivasvata Manu)․

Saryu River
Sarayu River is mentioned in ancient Hindu scriptures such as the Ved and Ramayan․ It is one of the seven tributaries of the river Gaṅgā and considered so sacred that it washes away impurities of humankind․ Ayodhya ‐ Birth place of Lord Shree Ram and capital of state Ayodhya in Treta Yuga is located on the bank of Sarayu River, thus it is highly significant in India․ Ram ki Paidi is the series of Ghat on Sarayu with a large stretch of ghats and gardens, along with row of temples․ It is visited by millions of devotees around the year and take a holy dip on various religious events and festivals․
The river Ghaghara is the main tributary of Sarayu River, also Mahakali and Karnali rivers join in the Bahraich District of Uttar Pradesh and then famous as Sarayu River․ Later Sarayu River meets sacred River Ganges․

The River Sarayu is closely associated with the Lord Shree Rama ‐ Seventh incarnation of Supreme Lord Vishnu as the city of Ayodhya is situated on the banks of River Sarayu․ King Dashratha had performed Putrakameshti Yagya on the northern bank of River Sarayu to get children․ Also on the banks of River Sarayu Shree Rama had received various secret mantra of Dhanurvidhya sage Vishwamitra ‐ one of the seven sage of current manavantara․ On the bank of the River Sarayu, King Dashratha accidentally killed Shravan Kumar, and Shravan‚s parents had cursed the king that he too will die from the sorrow of loss of son․ As per the legend Lord Shree Ram walked deep into Sarayu River and disappeared after completion of all action as seventh incarnation․ Soon at the same place Lord Vishnu appeared resting on Ananta Sesha and blessed his devotees․

The festival of Ram Navami and Diwali is celebrated every year with great zeal and millions of Diyas․ Sarayu Aarti is another key attraction in Ayodhya same like the Ganga Aarti of Haridwar․ Construction of magnificent temple of Lord Shree Ram is about to start at Ayodhya and also 251 mtrs tall statue of Shree Ram will be installed on bank of sacred River Sarayu․

Dashrath Mahal
Dashrath Mahal in Ayodhya is an extraordinary place to visit and that may still sound to be a mere understatement, such is the beauty and charm of this fascinating tourist spot․ It is situated 50m away from Hanuman garhi․ This place is far more than just being a tourist sightseeing spot․ A weekend at the Dashrath Mahal in Ayodhya is a rejuvenating and refreshing experience for anybody․ Incredible architecture and world ‐class standards make this place a wondrous and coveted one․ Absolute relaxation along with enjoyment is what you get for guarantee being at the Dashrath Mahal, Ayodhya․
Travellers can explore different interesting themes, great designs and various merchandise, all under a single and common roof at the near proximity stores of Dasharath Mahal․ It is indeed an astounding and scintillating place for visiting with friends and family members․ Dasharath Mahal also has a couple of other names by which it is known as; they are Badi Jagah and Bada Asthan․ It is believed that this is actually the place where the great, noble and benevolent king Dasharath who was also the father of Lord Rama from the masterpiece Indian epic Ramayana lived․ A very good restaurant known as Makan malai is located nearby the Dashrath Mahal where many of the travellers and tourists visiting go to fulfil their hunger for food․
